Detailed Description

DES. Triple DES. Originally designed by IBM and NSA in the 1970s.

Today, DES's 56-bit key renders it insecure to any well-resourced attacker. DESX and 3DES extend the key length, and are still thought to be secure, modulo the limitation of a 64-bit block. All are somewhat common in some industries such as finance. Avoid in new code.
